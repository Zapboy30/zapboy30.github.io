Street Trees

Infographics, Print

In this project I aimed to highlight the hidden relationships between each species of tree and highlight how truly diverse the tree population of the Kensington Market is. Toronto has all of their street trees mapped out by address and labelled by species. Plus, since horticulture is such an established field, there is plenty of data on each tree species that I could branch out with. I choose to focus on the Kensington Market area because its a neighbourhood that both Toronto natives and visitors would know of.

↓

I started out by trying to organize the trees using the shape of their leaves, but since there are so many species in the Kensington Market there wasn’t a good way to do this without making the page look cluttered. Plus, I could get more interesting information out of the data with a more abstract direction. I organized the data hierarchically with circles, using the size of the circles to represent the quantity of trees. I could’ve easily mapped the circles to a geographic map of the market, But I felt organizing them by genus, species, etc. was more interesting from a biodiversity perspective. A map could’ve been interesting, but it wouldn't have been able to visualize the hidden relationships between each type of trees as well.
