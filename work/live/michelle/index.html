A font to remember

Michelle is a font is designed in the tradition of beautiful oldstyle typefaces. I took a lot of inspiration in the making of the typeface from old literary works from the turn of the twentieth-century. The primary goal of this project was to create an oldstyle font that enabled comfortable long form reading on the web. I took stylistic queues from the calligraphic strokes of oldstyle typography and added a contemporary flair. The result was a font that is -- in my opinion -- a reflection of the atemporality of our current period in design history.

About the font

Michelle was made to serve all of the typographic needs of small personal internet blogs. The project was completed to fulfill the course requirements of the typeface design course at Sheridan college. Although the project is technically finished, I may or may not revisit the typeface again later on when I am more skillful at type design.
