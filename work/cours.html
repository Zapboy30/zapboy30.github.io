Cours

Branding, App Design

Cours is a running app that was made with runners in mind. It’s designed with simplicity, functionality, and self improvement in mind. At it’s core, Cours is a running app that doesn't just track you, it coaches you too. Cours provides core runners with training guides and workout recommendations that are modified to fit their needs using our space age AI technology.

↓

A running app that doesn't just track you, it coaches you too

In the app the user has the ability to input injuries and get recommended training schedule changes. They have the ability to set short or long term fitness goals. They get daily motivation through kind user reminders of their goals. The app has end-to-end encryption; only you have access to your data. Cours also has the ability to export the data from a user’s runs into spreadsheets. Cours keeps track of a runner’s average pace, stride, cadence, splits, heart rate, elevation, heart rate, and heart rate zones. Also Cours’ training plans are based on user's skill and experience. It is not a one size fits all app, this app allows users to input their average pace, experience level, etc, in order to get a specialized schedule suited to their specific situation.

The planning phase of this project involved two stages happening simultaneously; iterations of the app's user flows and planning the visual identity of the app. First I started with several very rough hand drawn user flow charts. Then I choose the best parts of each flow chart, and I began loosely sketching—to scale—barebones wireframes for the most important screens of each flow. This helped me to get a feeling of what the layout would look like to scale on a real iPhone. At the same time I began to collect visuals to which I thought could be interesting from both an aesthetic and functional perspective. Since this was the first time I've ever designed an app for mobile, I looked at what the apps I use on a day to day basis as both a form competitive analysis and inspiration. Eventually I decided on a rough layout for each page I began to organize all of the app's data as per the iOS Human Interface guidelines..
